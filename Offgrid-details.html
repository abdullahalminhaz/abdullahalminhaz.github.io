Design, Setup and Analysis of Off-Grid Solar System

A compact off-grid power system delivering reliable DC/AC supply up to 0.5kW

May 2024 – Jun 2024 • Renewable Energy Research Center (RERC) Project

Project Summary

This project focused on designing, installing and testing a small-scale off-grid solar power system capable of delivering both DC and AC loads up to ~0.5 kW. The system integrates photovoltaic panels, a charge controller, battery storage, power inversion (DC→AC), protection and monitoring. The main goal was to demonstrate continuous daytime operation and 4–6 hours of nighttime autonomy for essential loads in a remote or isolated setting.

System Demonstration

The demonstration video shows the installed off-grid setup during daytime operation and a short night test. The footage highlights array configuration, MPPT charge controller behavior, battery voltage profile, inverter output to AC loads, and system protections used to prevent deep discharge and overloads. Key performance indicators observed include daily energy yield, battery state-of-charge (SoC) behavior, and inverter efficiency under light loads.

Use the video to inspect wiring layout, mounting details, and how the system responds when switching between DC and AC loads. This practical evidence supports the measured run-time estimates and validates design assumptions.

Key Features

PV Array

Array sized for ~0.5 kW peak output with series-parallel arrangement for voltage & current optimization.

Battery Storage

Lead-acid / Li-ion battery bank sized to provide 4–6 hours autonomy at typical loads.

MPPT Controller

Maximum Power Point Tracking charge controller to maximize energy harvest and protect the battery.

Inverter & Protections

Pure/modified sine inverter with overcurrent, undervoltage, and thermal protections for appliances.

Hardware & Design

Major Components
  • Photovoltaic panels (~X × 250 W modules — configured to match MPPT voltage)
  • Battery bank (capacity sized for 4–6 hr of required load)
  • MPPT charge controller with battery management
  • Inverter (0.5 kW rated) for AC loads
  • Protection devices: fuses, circuit breakers, DC isolators
  • Mounting & cabling: tilted array mounts, proper gauge wiring
Design Decisions
  • Array sizing balanced to meet daily load and charge losses
  • Battery size selected using depth-of-discharge and autonomy targets
  • Over-provisioned protections for safety and longevity
  • Practical trade-offs: cost vs runtime vs footprint

Performance Summary

Measured system performance showed expected daytime energy production consistent with insolation, and battery discharge behaviour matched the calculated autonomy windows (approx. 4–6 hours for typical loads). Observed inverter efficiency was close to manufacturer specs under light to moderate loads. Key lessons included the importance of correct charge controller setpoints and practical considerations for wiring losses and thermal management of the inverter and panels.

Applications

  • Sustainable energy solutions for remote homes and community lighting
  • Educational demonstration for renewable-energy labs and workshops
  • Off-grid power for remote monitoring stations and telecom shelters
  • Agricultural pumping and irrigation support in off-grid farms
  • Industrial: Microgrids for light manufacturing, lighting and control systems in field operations
